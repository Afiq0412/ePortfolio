# 🌱 Semester 1

> My starting point in computer science: learning the basics, building confidence, and understanding how technology connects with people, logic, and communication.

## ✨ Semester Focus

Semester 1 was the beginning of my journey in computer science, where I started building the basic knowledge and learning habits needed for the semesters ahead. Through programming, digital logic, discrete mathematics, English communication, technology systems, and integrity-related subjects, I learned that computing is not only about writing code but also about thinking logically, communicating clearly, and making responsible decisions. This semester helped me become more comfortable with technical concepts that were new to me, especially programming fundamentals and the way computers process information at a basic level. It also trained me to be more disciplined in completing exercises, understanding lecture materials, and improving step by step through practice.

## 📝 Reflection

Overall, Semester 1 helped me adjust to university-level learning and gave me the confidence to continue in the field of computer science. I gained a stronger foundation in programming, logic, mathematics, and communication, which became important for later technical courses. I also learned that progress in computing requires patience because many concepts become clearer only after repeated practice and problem solving. This semester taught me to be more curious, more organized, and more willing to learn from mistakes. It became the starting point for my growth as a student who is not only learning technical skills, but also learning how to think, communicate, and act responsibly.
