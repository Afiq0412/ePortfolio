A1 : Project Proposal

PROBLEM 

The UTM Counseling Centre currently faces operational inefficiencies due to the fragmented management of posts and booking services across platforms like Google Forms, Facebook booking, and WhatsApp. For example, patients will contact them via WhatsApp for inquiries to schedule, details and confirm booked. This way is very complicated, not friendly to the user and also causes a lot of problems. 
This disjointed approach leads to three critical usability and user experience challenges. Firstly, there is a delay in clients booking appointments and receiving test results, hampering efficiency and timeliness. Secondly, the manual handling of counseling records and documentation introduces the risk of errors, data redundancy and inconsistency which will affect learnability and accuracy. Thirdly, the use of multiple forms in the current system contributes to data redundancy and inconsistency, impacting efficiency and overall user experience. The existing UTM Counseling Centre system also faces a challenge in its communication strategy. There is a lack of important notifications for both the UTM community and external participants regarding upcoming events or seminars. The absence of a systematic notification system diminishes the center's ability to engage with the community and outsiders effectively. This issue directly impacts the user experience goal of providing relevant information in a timely manner, hindering the promotion and participation in events and seminars hosted by the counseling center.

SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOALS (SDG)

SDG 3: GOOD HEALTH AND WELL-BEING

The inefficient booking system and delayed communication may impact the mental health and well-being of individuals seeking counseling services. Improving the efficiency of the counseling center’s operations can contribute to better health outcomes.

SDG 16 : PEACE , JUSTICE AND STRONG INSTITUTIONS

Ensuring a smooth and error-free counseling process contributes to the establishment of strong institutions. The implementation of a unified system can help in reducing errors, ensuring justice in service delivery, and promoting a peaceful environment for users seeking mental health support.

PROPOSED SOLUTION

UTM Counseling Centre : A path to emotional well-being

From the problem statement, we have come up with one proposed solution which is creating a comprehensive and integrated counseling system. The proposed solution involves 4 major components such as appointment management, communication management, data management and lastly, general considerations of the system.

  1. Appointment Management:
  • We will be developing a user-friendly portal for booking the appointment. For example ,the portal’s homepage features a straightforward “Book Appointment” button, immediately guiding users to the booking section. The booking process is simplified into a few easy steps, where users select their preferred counseling service from a clear menu and then choose a date and time from an intuitive calendar interface, similar to scheduling a ride.
  • Users will receive a confirmation notification through email once the booking is successful. Users will receive an immediate confirmation notification sent to their registered email address. This automated email serves as a concise acknowledgment, confirming the details of their appointment, including the chosen date and time. The email may also include additional information such as the counselor’s name and contact details, as well as any specific instructions or resources relevant to the upcoming session.
  • Real-time calendar availability of counselors will be displayed to enable users to book their slots accordingly and any booking changes will be reflected in the system.
  • Wearable appointment reminders for users who opting in can receive appointment reminders directly on their wearable devices. This ensures that they stay updated about their scheduled counseling sessions without relying solely on email notifications or portal access.
  1. Communication Management :
  • An integrated messaging system will be added for users to communicate with the counselor regarding their booking sessions or any inquiries.
  • Push notification will be installed to ensure both parties are alerted with the messages.
  • Wearable notifications for messages is utilizing wearables to receive push notifications for messages from counselors enhances communication accessibility. Users can be alerted via their wearables about new messages or updates regarding their sessions, promoting timely interaction.
  1. Data Management :
  • A single form system will be implemented. Users will fill out one comprehensive form rather than multiple separate ones, simplifying the data entry process. This system form will standardize the information gathered, minimizing data redundancy and ensuring consistency across user submissions.
  • Wearable input for mood tracking is implementing wearable compatibility for mood or emotional state tracking allows users to input their current feelings directly through their wearables. This data can be integrated into the system, providing a more comprehensive view of the user’s emotional state over time.

4. General Considerations :

  • Feedback form will be implemented to gather insights from users and counselors which can help for improvement and overall system.
  • The system will be scalable so that it will be easily modified and add new features without sacrificing performance.
  • Wearable based feedback function is users and counselors could provide feedback through the wearable interface, offering quick, on-the-go access to the feedback form. This accessibility can encourage more frequent and immediate input, contributing to continuous system improvement.

TARGET USERS

  1. UTM Community (Students and Staff):
    • The proposed centralized Counseling Centre System offers seamless appointment booking, faster test result processing, and automated notifications about upcoming events and seminars. For students and staff, this means efficient access to counseling services, reduced waiting times, and timely information on mental health resources and events. The user-friendly interface enhances their experience by streamlining the entire process, contributing to improved mental health and well-being.
  2. Administrative Staff:
    • The administrative staff benefit from the proposed system through streamlined data management. The system automates the recording of counseling sessions, eliminating the need for manual data entry and reducing the risk of errors. Centralized data storage enhances accessibility and retrieval, contributing to better organization and management of counseling records. The system’s efficiency supports administrative staff in their roles, aligning with SDG 8 (Decent Work and Economic Growth) by promoting productivity and reducing manual workload.
  3. Counselors:
    • Counselors gain several advantages from the proposed system, such as automated test score calculations, faster result generation, and centralized record management. This allows counselors to focus more on the counseling process itself rather than administrative tasks. The system’s ability to provide timely information on scheduled appointments and upcoming events enhances their ability to plan and prepare.
