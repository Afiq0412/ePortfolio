# 🚀 Semester 2

> A stronger technical semester where I started connecting programming, system design, statistics, hardware, ethics, and user experience.

## ✨ Semester Focus

Semester 2 expanded my foundation by introducing more structured and practical areas of computer science. I learned how computers work at a lower level through computer organization and architecture, while also improving my programming skills through more advanced C++ activities. At the same time, courses such as System Analysis and Design and Human Computer Interaction helped me understand that software should be planned carefully and designed around user needs. Probability and statistical data analysis introduced me to the importance of data in decision-making, while ethics reminded me that technical work must be guided by responsibility and professional values. This semester helped me connect technical theory with real assignments, diagrams, reports, and small projects.

## 📝 Reflection

Semester 2 showed me that software development is much bigger than writing code. I began to understand that a good system depends on many connected parts, including hardware behavior, user experience, data analysis, system requirements, and ethical responsibility. I gained stronger confidence in programming and also learned how to plan systems before implementation. The combination of technical and human-centered subjects helped me see computing from different angles. By the end of the semester, I became more aware that a future IT professional must not only solve technical problems, but also understand users, communicate designs clearly, and make decisions that are responsible and useful.
